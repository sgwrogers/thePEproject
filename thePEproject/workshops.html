HOW TO TEACH OUTSTANDING PE LESSONS

Level 1 - PE Teacher Certificate

Participants will explore strategies to enhance student engagement in PE lessons while gaining a deeper understanding of diverse teaching approaches. This includes examining various teaching styles and models in PE, enabling them to develop a comprehensive learning framework that integrates a range of evidence-based strategies rooted in established learning theories.

Level 2 - Advanced PE Instructor Certificate

Participants will develop a deeper understanding of how to motivate students in the 21st century by using strategies that promote autonomy, purpose, relatedness, and flow. They will learn how to differentiate lessons to their students needs to ensure inclusivity of all students. They will develop strategies to promote deeper-level thinking by learning how to ask good questions whilst also maximizing physical activity levels, and will improve their ability to assess and provide meaningful feedback.

Level 3 - Master PE Certificate

Participants will learn how to create a positive learning environment, manage student misbehavior in a calm, fair, and firm manner. They will also develop an understanding of how they can impact their students character, and promote virtues that transcends sports such as respect, resilience, kindness, fairness etc. Finally, they will learn how to speak into students worth, value and identity.

Scott Rogers

Course Instructor

Scott Rogers is an internationally experienced Physical Education teacher with over 15 years of experience. Scott is from the UK and has worked in international schools around the world, from London, Washington DC, Prague, and Chicago. He has a first-class degree in Physical Education Sports & Exercise Science, PGCE and a MA in International Education with Distinctions. He is also the author and creator of ‘The PE Project’ which provides resources and pedagogical articles to teachers, schools and universities around the world, having over 50,000 downloads and up to 7,000 monthly visitors. He has also published articles in the UK’s Association for Physical Education’s (AfPE) journal. He is passionate about supporting and equipping PE teachers to become more effective practitioners.
